# Chapter 10: Verbs of Change & Improvement

Business is about making things better, bigger, smaller, or different. These verbs describe change. They are strong words that show impact — perfect for reviews, self-appraisals, and proposals.

**How to read each entry:** the verb, a simple meaning, common word partners, two examples, and a workplace scenario line.

---

### enhance
- **Meaning:** to improve the quality or value of something.
- **Collocations:** enhance performance, quality, an experience, accuracy.
- **Examples:** We enhanced the model's accuracy. / This feature enhances the user experience.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We enhanced the dashboard so users can filter by region."

### improve
- **Meaning:** to make something better.
- **Collocations:** improve performance, quality, results, efficiency.
- **Examples:** We improved load time by 40%. / The change improved accuracy.
- **Scenario:** Stand-up: "I improved the query, so the report now runs in seconds."

### refine
- **Meaning:** to make small improvements to something.
- **Collocations:** refine a model, a process, an approach, an estimate.
- **Examples:** We refined the model over several rounds. / Let's refine the wording.
- **Scenario:** In planning: "We'll refine the model based on this week's feedback."

### revise
- **Meaning:** to change something to make it correct or better.
- **Collocations:** revise a plan, an estimate, a document, a forecast.
- **Examples:** We revised the forecast down. / Please revise the report.
- **Scenario:** In an email: "I've revised the plan based on your comments."

### restructure
- **Meaning:** to organise something in a new way.
- **Collocations:** restructure a team, a process, code, data.
- **Examples:** We restructured the pipeline. / The team was restructured.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We restructured the code so it's easier to maintain."

### transform
- **Meaning:** to change something completely.
- **Collocations:** transform a business, data, a process, results.
- **Examples:** The new tool transformed our workflow. / We transform raw data into features.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "This platform can transform how we make decisions."

### upgrade
- **Meaning:** to move to a better version.
- **Collocations:** upgrade a system, software, hardware, a plan.
- **Examples:** We upgraded the servers. / The library needs upgrading.
- **Scenario:** Stand-up: "I'm upgrading the framework to fix the security issue."

### accelerate
- **Meaning:** to make something happen faster.
- **Collocations:** accelerate growth, a process, delivery, progress.
- **Examples:** This tool accelerates model training. / We accelerated the timeline.
- **Scenario:** To stakeholders: "Automating this step accelerates delivery by a week."

### boost
- **Meaning:** to increase or improve.
- **Collocations:** boost sales, performance, morale, accuracy.
- **Examples:** The feature boosted engagement. / This boosts accuracy by 5%.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "The new data source boosted the model's accuracy."

### strengthen
- **Meaning:** to make something stronger.
- **Collocations:** strengthen a process, security, a relationship, controls.
- **Examples:** We strengthened data security. / This strengthens our case.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We strengthened the checks to catch bad data early."

### expand
- **Meaning:** to make something bigger or wider.
- **Collocations:** expand a team, coverage, scope, a market.
- **Examples:** We expanded the pilot to five countries. / The team is expanding.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "We'd like to expand the pilot to all regions."

### reduce
- **Meaning:** to make something smaller or less.
- **Collocations:** reduce cost, risk, time, errors.
- **Examples:** We reduced costs by 20%. / This reduces processing time.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "The change reduced errors by half."

### minimise
- **Meaning:** to make as small as possible.
- **Collocations:** minimise risk, cost, downtime, disruption.
- **Examples:** We minimised downtime during the update. / Let's minimise manual steps.
- **Scenario:** In planning: "We'll deploy at night to minimise disruption."

### mitigate
- **Meaning:** to make a bad thing less serious.
- **Collocations:** mitigate risk, impact, a problem, damage.
- **Examples:** We put controls in place to mitigate the risk. / This mitigates the impact of failures.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "To mitigate the risk, we added a backup pipeline."

### eliminate
- **Meaning:** to remove something completely.
- **Collocations:** eliminate errors, waste, a step, risk.
- **Examples:** We eliminated the manual step. / This eliminates duplicate records.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "Automation eliminated the weekly manual export."

### consolidate
- **Meaning:** to combine several things into one.
- **Collocations:** consolidate data, reports, systems, teams.
- **Examples:** We consolidated three reports into one. / Let's consolidate the data sources.
- **Scenario:** To your team: "I'm consolidating all the metrics into a single dashboard."

### simplify
- **Meaning:** to make something easier or less complex.
- **Collocations:** simplify a process, a model, a design, code.
- **Examples:** We simplified the workflow. / A simpler model is easier to explain.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We simplified the model without losing accuracy."

### standardise
- **Meaning:** to make things follow the same rules or format.
- **Collocations:** standardise a process, data, naming, formats.
- **Examples:** We standardised the naming across teams. / Let's standardise the report format.
- **Scenario:** To your team: "If we standardise the data format, joins get much easier."

### adapt
- **Meaning:** to change to fit a new situation.
- **Collocations:** adapt a plan, a model, an approach, to change.
- **Examples:** We adapted the model for the new market. / The team adapted quickly.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We adapted the approach after the first results came in."

### align
- **Meaning:** to make things agree or work together.
- **Collocations:** align goals, teams, a strategy, on a plan.
- **Examples:** Let's align on the priorities. / The plan aligns with company goals.
- **Scenario:** In a meeting: "Before we start, let's align on what success looks like."

### increase
- **Meaning:** to make something larger in amount.
- **Collocations:** increase revenue, accuracy, capacity, speed.
- **Examples:** We increased throughput by 30%. / This increases retention.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "The change increased sign-ups by 12%."

### decrease
- **Meaning:** to make something smaller in amount.
- **Collocations:** decrease cost, latency, churn, errors.
- **Examples:** We decreased latency significantly. / Churn decreased after the fix.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "The new onboarding decreased first-month churn."

### modernise
- **Meaning:** to update something to current standards.
- **Collocations:** modernise a system, a stack, infrastructure.
- **Examples:** We modernised the data stack. / The tools need modernising.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "Modernising the pipeline will cut maintenance time."

### overhaul
- **Meaning:** to change something completely to improve it.
- **Collocations:** overhaul a system, a process, a design.
- **Examples:** We overhauled the reporting system. / The process needs a full overhaul.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We overhauled the pipeline, and it's far more reliable now."

### revamp
- **Meaning:** to give something a fresh new form.
- **Collocations:** revamp a dashboard, a design, a process.
- **Examples:** We revamped the dashboard. / The UI was revamped.
- **Scenario:** To stakeholders: "We revamped the dashboard so it's easier to read."

### scale
- **Meaning:** to grow to handle more.
- **Collocations:** scale a system, a solution, operations, up/down.
- **Examples:** The solution scales to millions of users. / We need to scale the pipeline.
- **Scenario:** In planning: "Let's make sure the design scales before we launch widely."

### optimise (fine-tune)
- **Meaning:** to make small adjustments for best results.
- **Collocations:** fine-tune a model, settings, parameters.
- **Examples:** We fine-tuned the model's settings. / Let's fine-tune the thresholds.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "I fine-tuned the parameters and gained another 2% accuracy."

### rework
- **Meaning:** to do something again in a better way.
- **Collocations:** rework a plan, a design, a section.
- **Examples:** We reworked the failing part. / The estimate needs reworking.
- **Scenario:** Stand-up: "I'm reworking the join logic to fix the duplicates."

### tighten
- **Meaning:** to make something stricter or more controlled.
- **Collocations:** tighten controls, security, a deadline, rules.
- **Examples:** We tightened the validation rules. / Let's tighten access controls.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We tightened the checks so bad data can't get through."

### broaden
- **Meaning:** to make something wider in scope.
- **Collocations:** broaden scope, access, coverage, a search.
- **Examples:** We broadened the analysis to all regions. / Let's broaden the criteria.
- **Scenario:** In planning: "We'll broaden the study to include mobile users too."

### cut
- **Meaning:** to reduce sharply.
- **Collocations:** cut costs, time, waste, steps.
- **Examples:** We cut processing time in half. / The change cut costs sharply.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "Automation cut our reporting time from days to minutes."

### phase (out/in)
- **Meaning:** to remove or introduce gradually.
- **Collocations:** phase out an old system, phase in a new tool.
- **Examples:** We're phasing out the old model. / The new process will be phased in.
- **Scenario:** To your team: "We'll phase in the new pipeline over two sprints."

### redesign
- **Meaning:** to design something again in a new way.
- **Collocations:** redesign a system, a process, a schema.
- **Examples:** We redesigned the data model. / The workflow was redesigned.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We redesigned the schema so queries run much faster."

### convert
- **Meaning:** to change from one form to another.
- **Collocations:** convert data, a format, a file, leads.
- **Examples:** We convert the raw logs into features. / The tool converts CSV to Parquet.
- **Scenario:** In tech: "The step converts the raw data into a clean table."

### restore
- **Meaning:** to bring something back to a working state.
- **Collocations:** restore a service, data, a backup, access.
- **Examples:** We restored the service in minutes. / The backup restored the lost data.
- **Scenario:** During an incident: "We restored the service from the last backup."

### recover
- **Meaning:** to get back to normal after a problem.
- **Collocations:** recover from an outage, data, performance.
- **Examples:** The system recovered quickly. / We recovered the lost records.
- **Scenario:** In a postmortem: "The service recovered within ten minutes of the fix."

### stabilise
- **Meaning:** to make something steady and reliable.
- **Collocations:** stabilise a system, performance, a process.
- **Examples:** We stabilised the pipeline. / Performance has stabilised.
- **Scenario:** Stand-up: "After the fix, the nightly jobs have stabilised."

### future-proof
- **Meaning:** to design so it stays useful as things change.
- **Collocations:** future-proof a system, a design, a skill set.
- **Examples:** We future-proofed the design. / Let's future-proof the schema.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We future-proofed the pipeline so adding new sources is easy."

### innovate
- **Meaning:** to introduce new ideas or methods.
- **Collocations:** innovate on a product, a process, in a field.
- **Examples:** The team innovates constantly. / We innovated on the recommendation method.
- **Scenario:** In a self-review: "I innovated by testing a new modelling approach."

### elevate
- **Meaning:** to raise to a higher level.
- **Collocations:** elevate quality, standards, a brand, an experience.
- **Examples:** This elevates the quality of our reports. / We elevated our testing standards.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "These changes elevate the whole team's output quality."

---

**Chapter 10 count: 40 power verbs.** In self-reviews and proposals, these verbs prove impact. Say what you changed and by how much: "We reduced costs by 20%," "We accelerated delivery by a week," "We eliminated the manual step."
