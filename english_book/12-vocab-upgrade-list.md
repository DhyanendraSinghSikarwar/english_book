# Chapter 12: The Master Upgrade List — Simple → Professional

This is the heart of the vocabulary section. Below are common simple words and their professional upgrades. For each, you get the professional word(s) and a short before/after example.

**Important rule:** the professional word is not always better. Simple words are often clearer and warmer, especially in chat and casual talk. Upgrade when you write to senior people, clients, or in formal reports. Keep it simple when you want to sound human and friendly. A short note appears where the simple word is actually the better choice.

The list is organised A–Z by the simple word.

## When the SIMPLE word is better (read this carefully)

Professional words are not always the right choice. Overusing them makes you sound stiff, robotic, or even fake. Here is when to keep it simple:

- **In chat (Slack/Teams):** "Can you help?" is warmer than "Could you assist?" Keep chat casual.
- **With teammates you know well:** "Let's fix this" beats "Let's rectify this." Save formal words for clients.
- **When speaking:** "We got approval" sounds natural out loud. "We obtained approval" can sound heavy in speech.
- **To sound human:** "Sorry for the delay" is more sincere than "I regret the delay." Real warmth wins trust.
- **For clarity:** "use" is clearer than "utilise" in almost every case. Never use "utilise" just to sound clever.

**The golden rule:** Upgrade for formal writing, senior audiences, and reports. Keep it simple for chat, teammates, and speech. If a simple word says it clearly, you often don't need the fancy one.
