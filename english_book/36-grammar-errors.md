# Chapter 36: The 30 Grammar Errors Indian Speakers Make Most

Your grammar is probably already good — Indian English is fluent and rich. But a handful of specific patterns are different from international English, and fixing them gives you a big, quick upgrade. This chapter covers the 30 most common ones.

**Format for each:** wrong → right → why → practice sentences.

---

## Articles (a / an / the) — the #1 issue

Indian languages don't have articles, so this is the single most common area to polish.

**Rules of thumb:**
- Use **"a/an"** for one non-specific thing: "I built **a** model." Use "an" before a vowel sound: "an error," "an hour."
- Use **"the"** for a specific thing both people know: "**The** model we discussed is ready."
- Use **no article** for general plurals and uncountable nouns: "Models learn from data" (in general).

### Error 1
- ❌ "I will send you report."
- ✅ "I will send you **the** report." (specific report)
- **Why:** a specific, known thing takes "the."
- **Practice:** "I updated the dashboard." / "Please review the code." / "The meeting is at 3."

### Error 2
- ❌ "I am data scientist."
- ✅ "I am **a** data scientist."
- **Why:** singular job/role takes "a/an."
- **Practice:** "She's a manager." / "He's an engineer." / "I'm a newcomer here."

### Error 3
- ❌ "Data science is future."
- ✅ "Data science is **the** future." (or "is a growing field")
- **Why:** "the future" is a fixed phrase; general fields take no article.
- **Practice:** "AI is the future." / "Python is a popular language." / "Accuracy is important."

## Present continuous overuse

Indian English often uses "-ing" forms with state verbs that international English keeps simple.

### Error 4
- ❌ "I am knowing the answer."
- ✅ "I **know** the answer."
- **Why:** state verbs (know, understand, believe, want, like, need) are not used in the continuous.
- **Practice:** "I understand." / "I want more data." / "She believes it's ready."

### Error 5
- ❌ "I am having a car / a question / two brothers."
- ✅ "I **have** a car / a question / two brothers."
- **Why:** for possession, use simple "have," not "having." ("I'm having lunch" is fine — that's an action.)
- **Practice:** "I have a question." / "We have three models." / "She has experience."

### Error 6
- ❌ "I am having a doubt."
- ✅ "I **have a question**."
- **Why:** two fixes — use "have" not "having," and "question" not "doubt" (see Chapter 14).
- **Practice:** "I have a quick question." / "Can I ask something?" / "One question:..."

## Prepositions and extra words

### Error 7
- ❌ "discuss about the plan"
- ✅ "**discuss** the plan"
- **Why:** "discuss" already includes "about."
- **Practice:** "Let's discuss the results." / "We discussed the budget." / "I'll discuss it with her."

### Error 8
- ❌ "return back / revert back / repeat again"
- ✅ "**return** / **reply** / **repeat**"
- **Why:** "back/again" is redundant.
- **Practice:** "I'll return the file." / "Please repeat that." / "She replied quickly."

### Error 9
- ❌ "cope up with the workload"
- ✅ "**cope with** the workload"
- **Why:** no "up" after cope.
- **Practice:** "I can cope with it." / "He copes with pressure well."

### Error 10
- ❌ "reach to the office / reach at the office"
- ✅ "**reach** the office" / "**get to** the office"
- **Why:** "reach" a place takes no preposition; or use "get to."
- **Practice:** "I'll reach the venue by 9." / "When did you get to the office?"

### Error 11
- ❌ "I will revert to you."
- ✅ "I'll **get back to** you."
- **Why:** "revert" means undo internationally (see Chapter 14).
- **Practice:** "I'll get back to you tomorrow." / "Let me get back to you on that."

### Error 12
- ❌ "order for / request for (as verb) / discuss on"
- ✅ "**order** / **request** / **discuss**" (no preposition as verbs)
- **Why:** these verbs don't take "for/on." (But the noun "a request for X" is fine.)
- **Practice:** "I ordered the parts." / "I requested access." / "We discussed the plan."

## Question tags and emphasis

### Error 13
- ❌ "You sent it, isn't it?"
- ✅ "You sent it, **didn't you**?"
- **Why:** the tag must match the verb. Overusing "isn't it?" for everything is Indian English.
- **Practice:** "It's ready, isn't it?" / "You'll join, won't you?" / "They agreed, didn't they?"

### Error 14
- ❌ "I told you only. / He is like that only. / Today only I sent it."
- ✅ "I **told you**. / He's just like that. / I sent it **today**."
- **Why:** "only" as emphasis is Indian English; remove or rephrase.
- **Practice:** "I sent it today." / "That's just how it works." / "I said so."

### Error 15
- ❌ "Do it today itself. / Come here itself."
- ✅ "Do it **today**." / "Come **here**."
- **Why:** "itself" as emphasis is Indian English.
- **Practice:** "Finish it today." / "Meet me here." / "Send it now."

## Introductions and names

### Error 16
- ❌ "Myself Rahul."
- ✅ "**I'm Rahul**." / "My name is Rahul."
- **Why:** "myself" can't introduce you.
- **Practice:** "Hi, I'm Priya." / "I'm the new analyst." / "My name's Sam."

### Error 17
- ❌ "What is your good name?"
- ✅ "**What's your name?**" / "May I ask your name?"
- **Why:** "good name" is a translation from Hindi.
- **Practice:** "What's your name?" / "Sorry, I didn't catch your name."

## Countable / uncountable nouns

### Error 18
- ❌ "informations / feedbacks / equipments / softwares"
- ✅ "**information** / **feedback** / **equipment** / **software**" (no plural "s")
- **Why:** these are uncountable — no plural form.
- **Practice:** "some information," "useful feedback," "the equipment," "our software."

### Error 19
- ❌ "trainings / researches / staffs"
- ✅ "**training sessions** / **research** / **staff / staff members**"
- **Why:** use a countable word ("sessions") or keep it singular.
- **Practice:** "three training sessions," "our research shows," "the staff are helpful."

### Error 20
- ❌ "a lot of luggages / two luggages"
- ✅ "**a lot of luggage** / **two bags**"
- **Why:** "luggage" is uncountable; count the items ("bags").
- **Practice:** "I have some luggage." / "two suitcases."

## Verb tenses

### Error 21
- ❌ "I am working here since 2020."
- ✅ "I **have been working** here since 2020." / "I've worked here since 2020."
- **Why:** "since [point in time]" needs the present perfect.
- **Practice:** "I've been here since March." / "She's worked on it since Monday."

### Error 22
- ❌ "I am working here for 3 years." / "since 3 years"
- ✅ "I've **worked** here **for** 3 years."
- **Why:** "for + duration" with present perfect; and use "for," not "since," with a length of time.
- **Practice:** "I've studied it for a year." / "We've used it for months."

### Error 23
- ❌ "Yesterday I have finished the report."
- ✅ "Yesterday I **finished** the report."
- **Why:** a finished action at a specific past time takes the simple past, not present perfect.
- **Practice:** "I sent it yesterday." / "We launched last week." / "She left on Monday."

### Error 24
- ❌ "He is telling that it won't work."
- ✅ "He **says** (that) it won't work." / "He's saying it won't work."
- **Why:** use "say (that)"; "tell" needs a person object ("he told me that...").
- **Practice:** "She says it's ready." / "He told me it's done." / "They say it works."

### Error 25
- ❌ "If I will get time, I will do it."
- ✅ "If I **get** time, I'll do it."
- **Why:** don't use "will" in the "if" part of a conditional.
- **Practice:** "If it rains, we'll stay in." / "If the data is clean, we'll model it."

## Word choice and other patterns

### Error 26
- ❌ "prepone the meeting"
- ✅ "**bring the meeting forward** / **move it earlier**"
- **Why:** "prepone" is not international (see Chapter 14).
- **Practice:** "Let's move it earlier." / "Can we bring it forward?"

### Error 27
- ❌ "kindly do the needful"
- ✅ "**please take care of it** / **please proceed**"
- **Why:** Indian-English formula unknown abroad.
- **Practice:** "Please handle this." / "Could you take care of it?"

### Error 28
- ❌ "Please revert the same."
- ✅ "**Please reply.**" / "Please confirm this."
- **Why:** "revert" (wrong sense) + "the same" (Indian English).
- **Practice:** "Please confirm." / "Let me know." / "Please reply by Friday."

### Error 29
- ❌ "Two years back I joined."
- ✅ "**Two years ago** I joined."
- **Why:** use "ago" for past time, not "back."
- **Practice:** "A week ago." / "Long ago." / "Some time ago."

### Error 30
- ❌ "off the light / on the fan"
- ✅ "**turn off** the light / **turn on** the fan"
- **Why:** "off/on" alone aren't verbs; use "turn off/on" (or "switch off/on").
- **Practice:** "Turn off the screen share." / "Switch on the camera." / "Turn it off."

---

## How to fix these

Don't try to fix all 30 at once. Pick your top three (articles, "discuss about," and "revert" are great starting points), watch for them in your writing and speech for a week, then move to the next three. Reading and listening to a lot of international English — podcasts, articles, colleagues — will make the correct patterns feel natural over time.

---

**Chapter 36 summary.** These 30 patterns are the difference between fluent Indian English and fluent international English. The biggest wins are articles (a/an/the), dropping redundant words ("discuss about," "return back"), avoiding the continuous with state verbs ("I know," not "I am knowing"), and using the right tenses with "since/for/ago." Fix a few at a time and your written and spoken English will quickly sound more polished to European ears.
