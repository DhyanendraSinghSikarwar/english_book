# Chapter 24: Decisions & Closing

The end of a meeting is where value is captured or lost. If decisions aren't stated clearly and actions aren't assigned with owners and dates, the meeting was wasted. This chapter gives you the language to summarise decisions, assign actions, confirm understanding, close cleanly, and follow up — with full templates.

---

## 1. Summarising decisions

Before closing, restate what was decided. This catches misunderstandings while everyone's still there.

- "So we've agreed on three things: first, we run the pilot; second, it starts in two weeks; third, Priya owns the data."
- "Let me summarise where we landed:..."
- "To recap the decisions: we're going with option B, and we'll review in a month."
- "Just so it's clear for the notes: the decision is to proceed with the pilot."

**Tip:** Say the decisions out loud even if they feel obvious. People often leave a meeting with different ideas of what was agreed.

## 2. Assigning actions (owner + task + deadline)

Every action needs three things: **who**, **what**, and **by when**. Never leave an action without an owner.

- "Raj, could you take ownership of the data pipeline fix by Friday?"
- "Anna, you'll draft the pilot plan — let's say by end of next week?"
- "So, action one: Priya cleans the data by Wednesday. Action two: I book the review. Everyone clear?"
- "Let's assign owners: who wants to take the vendor follow-up?"

**Confirming acceptance:**
- "Does that work for you, Raj?"
- "Happy to own that, [Name]?"

**Tip:** Ask the owner to confirm the deadline. If they say "Friday's tight, Monday's safer," you've just avoided a missed deadline.

## 3. Confirming understanding

- "Just to make sure we're all on the same page:..."
- "Let me check I've captured everything correctly."
- "Is there anything I've missed or got wrong?"
- "Are we all aligned on the next steps?"

## 4. Closing the meeting

End on time and end cleanly.

- "That's everything on the agenda. Thanks for your time — I'll circulate the minutes today."
- "We're at time, so let's wrap up. Thank you all."
- "Great discussion. I'll send a summary with the actions by end of day."
- "Thanks, everyone. Speak soon."

**If you finish early (a gift — take it):**
- "We've covered everything faster than expected, so I'll give you ten minutes back. Thanks all."

**If you're out of time but not finished:**
- "We're at time and haven't covered everything. Let me send the rest by email, or we can find another 15 minutes."

## 5. The follow-up / minutes email

Sending clear minutes after the meeting is expected in Europe and makes you look organised. Keep it short: decisions and actions, not a transcript.

**Full minutes email template:**

> **Subject:** Notes & actions — Churn model review, 8 July
>
> Hi all,
>
> Thanks for a productive meeting. Here's a quick summary.
>
> **Decisions:**
> - We'll proceed with a 3-month pilot of the churn model.
> - The pilot starts on 22 July.
>
> **Actions:**
> | Action | Owner | Due |
> |---|---|---|
> | Clean and validate the pilot dataset | Priya | 15 July |
> | Draft the pilot plan | Anna | 18 July |
> | Book the mid-pilot review | [You] | This week |
>
> **Parked for later:** the question of model retraining frequency — we'll revisit after the pilot.
>
> Let me know if I've missed anything.
>
> Best,
> [Name]

**Why this works:**
- Decisions and actions are separate and scannable.
- Every action has an owner and a due date.
- "Parked" items are recorded so they aren't lost.
- It invites corrections ("let me know if I've missed anything").

## 6. Handling unfinished decisions

Sometimes you can't decide in the meeting. That's okay — capture the next step.

- "We're not ready to decide today. Let's gather the missing data and decide by Thursday."
- "Let's take this to email — I'll lay out the options and we'll pick one."
- "We need [person] in the room for this. I'll set up a short follow-up."

---

## Common Indian-English pitfalls in decisions and closing

- **"Kindly do the needful and update me."** → "Please take care of this and let me know."
- **"I will revert with the minutes."** → "I'll send the minutes." (Avoid "revert.")
- **"Please confirm the same by EOD."** → "Please confirm by end of day."
- **"Who will take the ownership?"** → "Who'll take ownership?" (No "the.")
- **"Action items are pending from your side."** → "There are a couple of actions still open with you." (Avoid "from your side.")

---

**Chapter 24 summary.** Close every meeting by summarising the decisions, assigning each action to an owner with a deadline, and confirming everyone agrees. Then send short, scannable minutes with a decisions-and-actions format. This is what separates a meeting that changes things from one that just fills time — and it's an easy way to build a reputation for being organised and reliable.
