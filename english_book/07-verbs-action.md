# Chapter 7: Verbs of Action & Delivery

Power verbs are strong, precise verbs that make you sound professional. Instead of "do" or "make," you use a verb that says exactly what you mean. This chapter covers verbs about doing, building, and delivering work.

**How to read each entry:** the verb, a simple meaning, common word partners (collocations), two example sentences, and a one-line workplace scenario.

---

### implement
- **Meaning:** to put a plan or idea into action.
- **Collocations:** implement a solution, a policy, a feature, changes, a model.
- **Examples:** We implemented the new pricing model last week. / The team is implementing the security fixes now.
- **Scenario:** In a sprint review: "We've implemented the caching layer, so the dashboard now loads twice as fast."

### execute
- **Meaning:** to carry out a plan; also to run code.
- **Collocations:** execute a plan, a strategy, a query, a trade.
- **Examples:** We executed the migration over the weekend. / The query executes in under a second.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "The rollout plan was executed on schedule with zero downtime."

### deliver
- **Meaning:** to provide a finished result.
- **Collocations:** deliver results, value, a project, on a promise.
- **Examples:** We delivered the report a day early. / The model delivers a 15% lift in accuracy.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We delivered the pipeline on time and within budget."

### deploy
- **Meaning:** to release software or a model into use.
- **Collocations:** deploy a model, to production, a release, resources.
- **Examples:** We deployed the new version this morning. / The model is deployed across three regions.
- **Scenario:** Stand-up: "I'll deploy the fix to production once tests pass."

### launch
- **Meaning:** to start something new publicly.
- **Collocations:** launch a product, a campaign, an initiative, a feature.
- **Examples:** We launched the recommendation engine in June. / The team launched a pilot in two markets.
- **Scenario:** To stakeholders: "We plan to launch the feature to all users next quarter."

### initiate
- **Meaning:** to formally begin something.
- **Collocations:** initiate a process, a review, a conversation, contact.
- **Examples:** We initiated a full data audit. / She initiated the discussion with legal.
- **Scenario:** In an email: "I've initiated the onboarding process for the new vendor."

### establish
- **Meaning:** to set up something that lasts, or to prove a fact.
- **Collocations:** establish a process, a baseline, trust, a relationship.
- **Examples:** We established a weekly review. / First, we established a performance baseline.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We established a clear baseline before testing the change."

### conduct
- **Meaning:** to carry out an organised activity.
- **Collocations:** conduct research, an experiment, an analysis, a survey, an interview.
- **Examples:** We conducted an A/B test over four weeks. / She conducted interviews with ten users.
- **Scenario:** To a client: "We conducted a thorough analysis before recommending a change."

### perform
- **Meaning:** to do a task; also how well something works.
- **Collocations:** perform a task, an analysis; perform well/poorly.
- **Examples:** We performed a full system check. / The model performs well on new data.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "The model performs better than the previous version on all metrics."

### achieve
- **Meaning:** to reach a goal through effort.
- **Collocations:** achieve a goal, a target, results, success.
- **Examples:** We achieved our quarterly target. / The team achieved a 20% cost saving.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "We achieved all three objectives set for this quarter."

### accomplish
- **Meaning:** to complete something successfully (a little more formal than "achieve").
- **Collocations:** accomplish a task, a goal, a mission.
- **Examples:** We accomplished the migration ahead of schedule. / There's a lot to accomplish this sprint.
- **Scenario:** In a self-review: "This year I accomplished the full automation of our reporting."

### facilitate
- **Meaning:** to make something easier, or to lead a session.
- **Collocations:** facilitate a workshop, a discussion, a process, collaboration.
- **Examples:** The new tool facilitates faster reviews. / She facilitated the planning workshop.
- **Scenario:** In a calendar invite: "Sofia will facilitate the brainstorming session."

### coordinate
- **Meaning:** to organise people or tasks to work together.
- **Collocations:** coordinate a team, efforts, a release, with stakeholders.
- **Examples:** I coordinated the launch across three teams. / We need to coordinate with marketing.
- **Scenario:** In an update: "I'm coordinating the release with the engineering and QA teams."

### streamline
- **Meaning:** to make a process simpler and faster.
- **Collocations:** streamline a process, a workflow, operations.
- **Examples:** We streamlined the approval process. / This tool streamlines data cleaning.
- **Scenario:** To your manager: "By streamlining the workflow, we cut reporting time in half."

### optimise
- **Meaning:** to make something as good or efficient as possible.
- **Collocations:** optimise performance, a query, costs, a model.
- **Examples:** We optimised the query and it now runs 10x faster. / The team optimised cloud costs.
- **Scenario:** Sprint review: "I optimised the pipeline, so nightly jobs finish an hour earlier."

### automate
- **Meaning:** to make a task run by itself.
- **Collocations:** automate a task, a report, a workflow, testing.
- **Examples:** We automated the weekly report. / Let's automate this manual step.
- **Scenario:** To your team: "I automated the data checks, so no one has to run them by hand."

### integrate
- **Meaning:** to combine systems so they work as one.
- **Collocations:** integrate systems, data, a tool, an API.
- **Examples:** We integrated the CRM with our database. / The model is integrated into the app.
- **Scenario:** In planning: "Next sprint we'll integrate the payment API."

### migrate
- **Meaning:** to move data or systems from one place to another.
- **Collocations:** migrate data, to the cloud, a database, users.
- **Examples:** We migrated the database to the cloud. / All users were migrated overnight.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "The migration to the new platform is complete."

### configure
- **Meaning:** to set up settings for a system.
- **Collocations:** configure a server, settings, an environment, access.
- **Examples:** I configured the new environment. / We configured alerts for failures.
- **Scenario:** Stand-up: "I'm configuring the pipeline to run every night at 2 a.m."

### maintain
- **Meaning:** to keep something working over time.
- **Collocations:** maintain a system, standards, a codebase, quality.
- **Examples:** We maintain three production models. / The team maintains high code quality.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We maintain the pipeline and fix issues within a day."

### build
- **Meaning:** to create something, step by step.
- **Collocations:** build a model, a tool, a dashboard, a pipeline, trust.
- **Examples:** We built a churn prediction model. / I'm building a new reporting dashboard.
- **Scenario:** Stand-up: "I'm building the data pipeline for the new project."

### develop
- **Meaning:** to create or improve something over time.
- **Collocations:** develop a solution, a feature, skills, a strategy.
- **Examples:** We developed a custom scoring method. / She's developing her leadership skills.
- **Scenario:** To your manager: "I'm developing a faster method for feature selection."

### produce
- **Meaning:** to make or create a result.
- **Collocations:** produce results, a report, output, insights.
- **Examples:** The analysis produced clear insights. / The model produces daily predictions.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "The pipeline produces a fresh report every morning."

### generate
- **Meaning:** to create data, ideas, or value automatically.
- **Collocations:** generate revenue, leads, reports, ideas, data.
- **Examples:** The feature generated €1M in new revenue. / The script generates test data.
- **Scenario:** To stakeholders: "This model generates ranked leads for the sales team."

### complete
- **Meaning:** to finish fully.
- **Collocations:** complete a task, a project, a review, training.
- **Examples:** We completed the audit on time. / I've completed the first version.
- **Scenario:** Stand-up: "I completed the data cleaning and started on modelling."

### finalise
- **Meaning:** to make something final and ready.
- **Collocations:** finalise a plan, a document, a decision, details.
- **Examples:** Let's finalise the plan by Friday. / We finalised the budget yesterday.
- **Scenario:** In an email: "Once you approve, I'll finalise the report and share it."

### handle
- **Meaning:** to deal with a task or situation.
- **Collocations:** handle a request, a problem, a client, data.
- **Examples:** I'll handle the vendor call. / The system handles millions of records.
- **Scenario:** To your team: "I'll handle the client's questions in tomorrow's meeting."

### manage
- **Meaning:** to control or be in charge of something.
- **Collocations:** manage a project, a team, risk, expectations, time.
- **Examples:** She manages the analytics team. / We need to manage the client's expectations.
- **Scenario:** In a one-on-one: "I'm managing three projects this quarter."

### undertake
- **Meaning:** to take on a task or responsibility (formal).
- **Collocations:** undertake a project, a review, an analysis.
- **Examples:** We undertook a major review of the data. / The team undertook the migration.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We undertook a detailed study of customer behaviour."

### drive
- **Meaning:** to push something forward and make it happen.
- **Collocations:** drive growth, change, results, a project, adoption.
- **Examples:** This feature drives user engagement. / She drives the whole initiative.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "This project drives a measurable increase in retention."

### enable
- **Meaning:** to make something possible.
- **Collocations:** enable teams, growth, access, a feature.
- **Examples:** The new tool enables faster decisions. / This enables real-time reporting.
- **Scenario:** In a proposal: "This platform enables the team to self-serve their data."

### support
- **Meaning:** to help or provide backing.
- **Collocations:** support a team, a decision, a system, a claim.
- **Examples:** The data supports our conclusion. / I'll support you on this task.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "The results support the decision to expand the pilot."

### ensure
- **Meaning:** to make certain something happens.
- **Collocations:** ensure quality, accuracy, compliance, success.
- **Examples:** We ensure the data is accurate before release. / Please ensure the tests pass.
- **Scenario:** In an email: "I'll ensure the report is ready before the meeting."

### provide
- **Meaning:** to give or supply something.
- **Collocations:** provide support, feedback, data, a solution.
- **Examples:** I'll provide the numbers by noon. / The team provides ongoing support.
- **Scenario:** To a stakeholder: "We provide a weekly summary of key metrics."

### complete (roll out)
- **Meaning:** to release something gradually to everyone.
- **Collocations:** roll out a feature, a policy, an update.
- **Examples:** We rolled out the change to all users. / The update rolls out next week.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "We'll roll out the new dashboard team by team."

### spearhead
- **Meaning:** to lead an important effort.
- **Collocations:** spearhead a project, an initiative, a campaign.
- **Examples:** She spearheaded the AI adoption effort. / I spearheaded the data quality drive.
- **Scenario:** In a self-review: "I spearheaded the move to automated testing."

### orchestrate
- **Meaning:** to organise many parts into one smooth result.
- **Collocations:** orchestrate a launch, a workflow, pipelines, services.
- **Examples:** We orchestrate the jobs with a scheduler. / She orchestrated the whole event.
- **Scenario:** In tech: "The tool orchestrates all the data jobs in the right order."

### fulfil
- **Meaning:** to meet a requirement or promise.
- **Collocations:** fulfil a requirement, a role, a promise, an obligation.
- **Examples:** The solution fulfils all the requirements. / We fulfilled our commitment to the client.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "This design fulfils every requirement from the brief."

### realise
- **Meaning:** to make something real; to gain a benefit.
- **Collocations:** realise value, benefits, savings, a goal.
- **Examples:** We realised €500K in savings. / The benefits will be realised next year.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "We realised the full savings within six months."

### expedite
- **Meaning:** to make something happen faster.
- **Collocations:** expedite a process, a request, delivery, approval.
- **Examples:** Can we expedite the approval? / We expedited the shipment.
- **Scenario:** In an email: "Could you help expedite the access request? It's blocking the launch."

---

**Chapter 7 count: 40 power verbs.** Swap weak verbs for these in your next update. Instead of "we did the migration," say "we executed the migration." Instead of "we made the report faster," say "we optimised the report."
