# Bonus 2: 120 More Power Verbs

These verbs are **additional** to the 200 in Part 2 (Chapters 7–11) — none are repeated here. They are precise, professional verbs that make your writing and speech sound sharp and senior.

**Format:** the verb — simple meaning. *Example:* a workplace sentence.

---

## Action & delivery

1. **accommodate** — make room for; adjust to. *Example:* "We can accommodate the extra request next sprint."
2. **administer** — manage or run. *Example:* "She administers the access system."
3. **assemble** — bring together. *Example:* "We assembled a small task force."
4. **attain** — achieve or reach. *Example:* "We attained the target ahead of time."
5. **commission** — order or authorise work. *Example:* "We commissioned an external audit."
6. **craft** — make carefully. *Example:* "She crafted a clear proposal."
7. **engineer** — design or bring about. *Example:* "We engineered a faster pipeline."
8. **execute** *(reinforce)* — carry out. *Example:* "We executed the plan on schedule."
9. **furnish** — supply or provide. *Example:* "Please furnish the figures by Friday."
10. **institute** — set up or introduce. *Example:* "We instituted a weekly review."
11. **mobilise** — organise for action. *Example:* "We mobilised the team quickly."
12. **operationalise** — put into daily practice. *Example:* "Let's operationalise the new policy."
13. **prototype** — build an early version. *Example:* "We prototyped the feature in a week."
14. **provision** — set up (resources). *Example:* "We provisioned the servers overnight."
15. **render** — provide; cause to be. *Example:* "The tool renders the data as charts."
16. **repurpose** — use for a new purpose. *Example:* "We repurposed the old script."
17. **spearhead** *(reinforce)* — lead an effort. *Example:* "She spearheaded the migration."
18. **sustain** — maintain over time. *Example:* "We can sustain this pace for a month."
19. **terminate** — end. *Example:* "We terminated the failing job."
20. **triage** — prioritise by urgency. *Example:* "Let's triage the bugs first."

## Thinking & analysis

21. **appraise** — assess value or performance. *Example:* "We appraised each option carefully."
22. **ascertain** — find out for certain. *Example:* "We need to ascertain the cause."
23. **calibrate** — adjust precisely. *Example:* "We calibrated the model's thresholds."
24. **capture** — record or take in (data). *Example:* "The system captures every click."
25. **characterise** — describe the nature of. *Example:* "How would you characterise the trend?"
26. **corroborate** — confirm with evidence. *Example:* "A second test corroborated the result."
27. **decipher** — work out something unclear. *Example:* "We deciphered the messy log format."
28. **discern** — perceive or detect. *Example:* "We can discern a weekly pattern."
29. **extrapolate** — extend from known data. *Example:* "Don't extrapolate too far from a small sample."
30. **parse** — analyse into parts. *Example:* "The script parses the raw text."
31. **pinpoint** — locate exactly. *Example:* "We pinpointed the source of the error."
32. **probe** — investigate deeply. *Example:* "Let's probe the outliers."
33. **quantify** *(reinforce)* — measure in numbers. *Example:* "Can we quantify the impact?"
34. **simulate** — imitate or model. *Example:* "We simulated peak traffic."
35. **substantiate** — support with evidence. *Example:* "The data substantiates the claim."
36. **survey** — examine broadly. *Example:* "We surveyed the available tools."
37. **tabulate** — arrange in a table. *Example:* "Let's tabulate the results."
38. **visualise** — represent visually. *Example:* "We visualised the churn by segment."
39. **vet** — check carefully. *Example:* "We vetted the data before modelling."
40. **contextualise** — put in context. *Example:* "Let me contextualise these numbers."

## Communication & influence

41. **advocate** — publicly support. *Example:* "I advocate for cleaner data standards."
42. **articulate** *(reinforce)* — express clearly. *Example:* "She articulated the risk well."
43. **circulate** — send around. *Example:* "I'll circulate the notes today."
44. **demonstrate** — show clearly. *Example:* "The results demonstrate real value."
45. **depict** — represent or show. *Example:* "This chart depicts the trend."
46. **disclose** — reveal. *Example:* "We disclosed the limitations upfront."
47. **disseminate** — spread widely. *Example:* "We disseminated the findings across teams."
48. **elicit** — draw out a response. *Example:* "The survey elicited useful feedback."
49. **exemplify** — be a typical example of. *Example:* "This case exemplifies the problem."
50. **frame** — present in a particular way. *Example:* "Let's frame it as a business win."
51. **illustrate** — make clear with examples. *Example:* "Let me illustrate with a quick example."
52. **portray** — depict or describe. *Example:* "The report portrays a healthy trend."
53. **signpost** — guide or indicate direction. *Example:* "I'll signpost the key sections."
54. **solicit** — ask for. *Example:* "We solicited input from all teams."
55. **specify** — state exactly. *Example:* "Please specify the exact columns."
56. **stipulate** — require as a condition. *Example:* "The contract stipulates monthly reviews."
57. **underscore** — emphasise. *Example:* "The results underscore the need to act."
58. **underpin** — support or be the basis of. *Example:* "Solid data underpins the model."
59. **reaffirm** — state again firmly. *Example:* "I want to reaffirm our commitment."
60. **reiterate** *(reinforce)* — say again. *Example:* "Let me reiterate the deadline."

## Change & improvement

61. **amplify** — increase or strengthen. *Example:* "Good onboarding amplifies retention."
62. **augment** — add to; increase. *Example:* "We augmented the data with new features."
63. **bolster** — strengthen or support. *Example:* "Extra checks bolster data quality."
64. **cement** — make firm or permanent. *Example:* "This win cemented our approach."
65. **condense** — make shorter or denser. *Example:* "Let's condense the report to one page."
66. **curtail** — cut short or reduce. *Example:* "We curtailed the scope to save time."
67. **distil** — extract the essence. *Example:* "We distilled it into three points."
68. **fortify** — strengthen. *Example:* "We fortified the system against failures."
69. **galvanise** — spur into action. *Example:* "The results galvanised the team."
70. **hone** — sharpen or refine (a skill). *Example:* "She honed her presentation skills."
71. **rationalise** — make more efficient; justify. *Example:* "We rationalised the reporting suite."
72. **rectify** — put right. *Example:* "We rectified the data error."
73. **reinforce** — strengthen. *Example:* "This reinforces our earlier finding."
74. **reinstate** — restore. *Example:* "We reinstated the old check."
75. **revitalise** — give new life to. *Example:* "The redesign revitalised the dashboard."
76. **streamline** *(reinforce)* — make efficient. *Example:* "We streamlined the workflow."
77. **supplement** — add to. *Example:* "We supplemented the data with surveys."
78. **surpass** — exceed. *Example:* "We surpassed the target."
79. **unify** — bring together. *Example:* "We unified the data sources."
80. **synchronise** — coordinate timing. *Example:* "We synchronised the jobs."

## Management, planning & governance

81. **allocate** *(reinforce)* — give out resources. *Example:* "We allocated two people to it."
82. **anchor** — fix firmly; base on. *Example:* "Let's anchor the plan to real data."
83. **anticipate** — expect and prepare for. *Example:* "We anticipate higher load next week."
84. **apportion** — divide and assign. *Example:* "We apportioned the budget by team."
85. **arbitrate** — settle a dispute neutrally. *Example:* "She arbitrated the disagreement."
86. **broker** — arrange (a deal). *Example:* "He brokered the agreement between teams."
87. **cascade** — pass down through levels. *Example:* "We cascaded the update to all teams."
88. **codify** — put into a formal system. *Example:* "We codified our data standards."
89. **constrain** — limit or restrict. *Example:* "Time constrains what we can do."
90. **curate** — select and organise. *Example:* "We curated a set of key metrics."
91. **dedicate** — give fully to. *Example:* "We dedicated a sprint to clean-up."
92. **delineate** — define clearly. *Example:* "Let's delineate each person's role."
93. **designate** — assign or name officially. *Example:* "We designated a single owner."
94. **earmark** — set aside for a purpose. *Example:* "We earmarked funds for tooling."
95. **envision** — imagine or plan for the future. *Example:* "We envision a fully automated pipeline."
96. **equip** — provide with what's needed. *Example:* "We equipped the team with training."
97. **incentivise** — motivate with rewards. *Example:* "We incentivised early adoption."
98. **instil** — gradually build in (values). *Example:* "We instilled a culture of testing."
99. **leverage** *(reinforce)* — use to advantage. *Example:* "We leverage cloud tools for scale."
100. **marshal** — gather and organise. *Example:* "We marshalled the evidence for the case."
101. **moderate** — make less extreme; chair. *Example:* "She moderated the discussion."
102. **navigate** — find a way through. *Example:* "We navigated the approval process."
103. **nurture** — care for and develop. *Example:* "We nurture junior talent."
104. **offset** — balance or counter. *Example:* "Savings here offset costs elsewhere."
105. **preclude** — prevent or rule out. *Example:* "Budget limits preclude a full rebuild."
106. **prescribe** — recommend or lay down. *Example:* "The policy prescribes monthly retraining."
107. **preserve** — keep safe or maintain. *Example:* "We preserve the raw data."
108. **ratify** — formally approve. *Example:* "The board ratified the plan."
109. **reconcile** — make consistent or agree. *Example:* "We reconciled the two reports."
110. **regulate** — control with rules. *Example:* "We regulate who can access the data."
111. **safeguard** — protect. *Example:* "We safeguard customer data carefully."
112. **sanction** — approve; also penalise. *Example:* "Leadership sanctioned the extra spend."
113. **shortlist** — reduce to a final list. *Example:* "We shortlisted three vendors."
114. **tailor** — customise. *Example:* "We tailored the report to each team."
115. **uphold** — maintain or support. *Example:* "We uphold strict quality standards."
116. **withhold** — hold back. *Example:* "We withheld release until tests passed."
117. **yield** — produce; give way. *Example:* "The new method yields better results."
118. **incur** — bring on oneself (costs). *Example:* "We'd incur extra costs by rushing."
119. **accrue** — build up over time. *Example:* "Benefits accrue as more teams adopt it."
120. **avert** — prevent. *Example:* "Early detection averted a bigger failure."

---

**Total: 120 power verbs** (a few marked *reinforce* echo Part 2 for revision; the rest are new). With Part 2, you now have over 300 professional verbs. The skill is precision — pick the verb that says exactly what you did, and your work will sound as strong as it is.
