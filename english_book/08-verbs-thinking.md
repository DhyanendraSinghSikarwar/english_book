# Chapter 8: Verbs of Thinking & Analysis

These verbs describe how you think, study, and reach conclusions. For a data scientist, they are gold: they let you explain your analysis clearly and sound rigorous. Use them in reports, reviews, and when you present findings.

**How to read each entry:** the verb, a simple meaning, common word partners, two examples, and a workplace scenario line.

---

### analyse
- **Meaning:** to study something in detail to understand it.
- **Collocations:** analyse data, trends, results, behaviour.
- **Examples:** We analysed six months of sales data. / I'm analysing why churn went up.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We analysed the data and found three clear drivers of churn."

### assess
- **Meaning:** to judge the quality, size, or value of something.
- **Collocations:** assess risk, impact, performance, a situation.
- **Examples:** We assessed the risk before launching. / Let's assess the impact first.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "We assessed the impact and it affects about 5% of users."

### evaluate
- **Meaning:** to carefully judge value or effectiveness.
- **Collocations:** evaluate options, a model, performance, a vendor.
- **Examples:** We evaluated three tools. / The model was evaluated on new data.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We evaluated four models and chose the most accurate one."

### examine
- **Meaning:** to look at something closely.
- **Collocations:** examine data, evidence, a pattern, a case.
- **Examples:** We examined the outliers. / Let's examine the failed cases.
- **Scenario:** In an analysis: "We examined each error and found a common cause."

### investigate
- **Meaning:** to look into something to find the truth.
- **Collocations:** investigate an issue, a cause, a drop, an anomaly.
- **Examples:** We investigated the sudden drop in traffic. / I'm investigating the data mismatch.
- **Scenario:** Stand-up: "I'm investigating why the numbers don't match the source."

### determine
- **Meaning:** to find out or decide something for certain.
- **Collocations:** determine the cause, the impact, a value, eligibility.
- **Examples:** We determined the root cause. / The test determines which version wins.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We determined that the outage caused the data gap."

### identify
- **Meaning:** to find and name something.
- **Collocations:** identify a problem, a trend, an opportunity, a risk.
- **Examples:** We identified three key drivers. / The model identifies high-risk customers.
- **Scenario:** To stakeholders: "We identified the customers most likely to leave."

### interpret
- **Meaning:** to explain what something means.
- **Collocations:** interpret results, data, findings, a chart.
- **Examples:** Let me interpret these results for you. / The graph can be interpreted two ways.
- **Scenario:** In a presentation: "Let me help interpret what this spike actually means."

### estimate
- **Meaning:** to give an approximate value or judgment.
- **Collocations:** estimate cost, time, size, impact, effort.
- **Examples:** We estimate the effort at two weeks. / I estimated the savings at €200K.
- **Scenario:** In planning: "We estimate the model will take three weeks to build and test."

### forecast
- **Meaning:** to predict future values, usually with data.
- **Collocations:** forecast demand, sales, revenue, growth.
- **Examples:** We forecast a 10% rise in demand. / The model forecasts next quarter's sales.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "Our model forecasts demand two months ahead."

### predict
- **Meaning:** to say what will happen in the future.
- **Collocations:** predict an outcome, churn, behaviour, a value.
- **Examples:** The model predicts which users will churn. / We can't predict this precisely.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "The model predicts churn with 85% accuracy."

### infer
- **Meaning:** to work something out from evidence.
- **Collocations:** infer a cause, a relationship, meaning, intent.
- **Examples:** We can infer intent from clicks. / From the data, we infer a seasonal effect.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We infer that the price change caused the drop, though we can't prove it."

### derive
- **Meaning:** to get or calculate something from a source.
- **Collocations:** derive a value, a metric, a feature, insights.
- **Examples:** We derive the score from three inputs. / New features were derived from the raw logs.
- **Scenario:** In tech: "The risk score is derived from payment history and usage."

### validate
- **Meaning:** to check that something is correct or true.
- **Collocations:** validate results, data, an assumption, a model.
- **Examples:** We validated the results with a hold-out set. / Please validate the input data.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We validated the model on data it had never seen."

### verify
- **Meaning:** to confirm something is true or accurate.
- **Collocations:** verify a figure, a claim, data, identity.
- **Examples:** Let me verify the numbers first. / We verified the totals against the source.
- **Scenario:** In an email: "I'll verify the figures and confirm by end of day."

### quantify
- **Meaning:** to measure something in numbers.
- **Collocations:** quantify impact, risk, benefit, cost.
- **Examples:** Can we quantify the benefit? / We quantified the loss at €50K a month.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "We quantified the impact: it saves 200 hours a month."

### benchmark
- **Meaning:** to compare against a standard or others.
- **Collocations:** benchmark performance, against competitors, a model.
- **Examples:** We benchmarked our model against the baseline. / Let's benchmark our speed.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We benchmarked our results against last year's model."

### correlate
- **Meaning:** to show a relationship between two things.
- **Collocations:** correlate with, strongly/weakly, two variables.
- **Examples:** Sales correlate with ad spend. / These two metrics don't correlate.
- **Scenario:** In an analysis: "Usage correlates strongly with renewal — heavy users rarely leave."

### hypothesise
- **Meaning:** to suggest an idea to test.
- **Collocations:** hypothesise that, a cause, a relationship.
- **Examples:** We hypothesised that price drove churn. / Let's hypothesise and then test it.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We hypothesised that slow load times caused drop-off, then tested it."

### conclude
- **Meaning:** to reach a final judgment.
- **Collocations:** conclude that, from the data, an analysis.
- **Examples:** We conclude that the change worked. / From this, we conclude the risk is low.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We conclude that the new model is ready for production."

### explore
- **Meaning:** to look into something to learn more.
- **Collocations:** explore options, data, a hypothesis, possibilities.
- **Examples:** We explored several approaches. / Let's explore the data before modelling.
- **Scenario:** In planning: "First we'll explore the data to understand its shape."

### review
- **Meaning:** to look over something carefully.
- **Collocations:** review a document, code, results, performance.
- **Examples:** Please review the report. / We reviewed the model's errors.
- **Scenario:** In an email: "Could you review the analysis before I share it widely?"

### diagnose
- **Meaning:** to find the cause of a problem.
- **Collocations:** diagnose an issue, a fault, a cause, a bottleneck.
- **Examples:** We diagnosed the slowdown. / Let me diagnose why it's failing.
- **Scenario:** Stand-up: "I diagnosed the bottleneck — it's the join on the large table."

### measure
- **Meaning:** to find the size or amount of something.
- **Collocations:** measure impact, success, performance, results.
- **Examples:** We measure success by retention. / How do we measure this?
- **Scenario:** To stakeholders: "We'll measure success by the reduction in churn."

### compare
- **Meaning:** to look at differences and similarities.
- **Collocations:** compare options, results, models, periods.
- **Examples:** We compared the two approaches. / Let's compare this quarter to last.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "We compared both models and the new one wins on speed."

### calculate
- **Meaning:** to work out a number using math.
- **Collocations:** calculate a value, a rate, cost, probability.
- **Examples:** We calculated the churn rate. / I'll calculate the exact savings.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We calculated a monthly saving of €12,000."

### model
- **Meaning:** to build a math representation of something.
- **Collocations:** model behaviour, demand, risk, a process.
- **Examples:** We modelled customer behaviour. / The team models risk for each loan.
- **Scenario:** In planning: "We'll model demand to plan inventory better."

### uncover
- **Meaning:** to find something hidden.
- **Collocations:** uncover a pattern, an issue, insights, fraud.
- **Examples:** The analysis uncovered a data leak. / We uncovered a hidden trend.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "Our analysis uncovered a pattern no one had noticed."

### observe
- **Meaning:** to notice or watch something.
- **Collocations:** observe a pattern, a trend, behaviour, an effect.
- **Examples:** We observe a spike every Monday. / We observed no change after the update.
- **Scenario:** In an analysis: "We observe a strong weekend drop in activity."

### reveal
- **Meaning:** to show something that was not clear before.
- **Collocations:** reveal a trend, an insight, a gap, a cause.
- **Examples:** The data reveals a clear pattern. / The study revealed a training gap.
- **Scenario:** To stakeholders: "The analysis reveals that most churn happens in month three."

### scrutinise
- **Meaning:** to examine very closely and critically.
- **Collocations:** scrutinise data, results, a claim, spending.
- **Examples:** We scrutinised every assumption. / The board scrutinised the numbers.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We scrutinised the outliers to rule out data errors."

### deduce
- **Meaning:** to reach a conclusion by logic.
- **Collocations:** deduce a cause, a rule, an answer.
- **Examples:** From the pattern, we deduced the cause. / We can deduce the missing value.
- **Scenario:** In an analysis: "We deduced that the gap came from a failed nightly job."

### gauge
- **Meaning:** to estimate or judge something roughly.
- **Collocations:** gauge interest, reaction, size, feasibility.
- **Examples:** Let's gauge user interest first. / It's hard to gauge the impact yet.
- **Scenario:** In planning: "We ran a small test to gauge how users respond."

### project
- **Meaning:** to estimate a future figure based on current data.
- **Collocations:** project revenue, growth, costs, demand.
- **Examples:** We project 15% growth. / Costs are projected to fall next year.
- **Scenario:** To leadership: "We project the savings to reach €1M within a year."

### segment
- **Meaning:** to divide into meaningful groups.
- **Collocations:** segment customers, data, a market, users.
- **Examples:** We segmented users by behaviour. / Let's segment the market first.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We segmented customers into three risk groups."

### profile
- **Meaning:** to describe the key features of something.
- **Collocations:** profile a customer, a dataset, usage, performance.
- **Examples:** We profiled the typical churner. / Let's profile the dataset first.
- **Scenario:** In an analysis: "We profiled the data and found many missing values."

### aggregate
- **Meaning:** to combine numbers into totals or groups.
- **Collocations:** aggregate data, results, by region, by month.
- **Examples:** We aggregate sales by region. / The report aggregates daily data into weeks.
- **Scenario:** In tech: "We aggregate the events into hourly totals for the dashboard."

### classify
- **Meaning:** to sort into categories.
- **Collocations:** classify data, emails, risk, images.
- **Examples:** The model classifies emails as spam or not. / We classify users by activity.
- **Scenario:** To stakeholders: "The model classifies each ticket by urgency."

### rank
- **Meaning:** to put in order by importance or value.
- **Collocations:** rank options, leads, features, risks.
- **Examples:** We rank leads by likelihood to buy. / Let's rank the features by importance.
- **Scenario:** In a review: "The model ranks customers so sales can call the best ones first."

### attribute
- **Meaning:** to say what caused a result.
- **Collocations:** attribute a change to, growth to, a drop to.
- **Examples:** We attribute the growth to the new feature. / The drop is attributed to the outage.
- **Scenario:** In a report: "We attribute most of the lift to the improved onboarding."

---

**Chapter 8 count: 40 power verbs.** These verbs show rigour. In your next analysis, be precise: don't just "look at" the data — say you "analysed," "examined," or "scrutinised" it, depending on how deep you went.
